Section 02 — Audit Findings

Fiscal Stress Despite Strong GSDP Growth

Kerala's GSDP grew 11.97% yet revenue and fiscal deficits widened. Outstanding liabilities breached KFR Act targets as GoI grants collapsed 55.9%.

Filter
Severity 3 of 3 findings
Finding 01 — Fiscal Deficit
₹34,258Cr
Fiscal Deficit rose ₹8,704 Cr in 2023-24

Fiscal Deficit at 2.99% of GSDP. Borrowed funds used mainly for current consumption rather than capital creation, per CAG finding.

Finding 02 — Debt Overshoot
37.84%
Overall Liabilities/GSDP breaches KFR Act

Including off-budget ₹32,942 Cr via KIIFB & KSSPL — exceeding the KFR Act ceiling of 33.70% by 4.14 percentage points.

Finding 03 — Central Grants Crash
-55.9%
GoI Grants fell from ₹27,378 Cr to ₹12,068 Cr

Steepest drop in 5 years — causing revenue deficit to nearly double to ₹18,140 Cr, a 96.6% jump year-on-year.

Section 05 — Debt & Liabilities

Debt Structure & KFR Act Breaches

Total outstanding liabilities reach ₹4,15,221 Cr — including hidden off-budget borrowings through KIIFB and KSSPL that push the ratio beyond legal limits.

Total Outstanding Liabilities (Finance Accounts)
₹4,15,221 Cr

36.23% of GSDP — primary borrowings, internal debt, and small savings. Growing from ₹2,72,567 Cr in 2019-20 at a CAGR of 11.1%.
